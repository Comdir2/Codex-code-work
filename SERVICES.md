# Optional professional services

Code Work is MIT-licensed and can be used without purchasing anything. Organizations that want help adapting the workflow to their repositories may request a separately scoped professional engagement.

## Possible engagements

- **Repository onboarding:** map repository instructions, entry points, test commands, risk boundaries, and completion evidence into a practical Codex workflow.
- **Implementation guardrails:** define scope templates, protected surfaces, approval gates, and safe adaptive-parallelism rules for a codebase.
- **Test-backed change delivery:** implement an explicitly approved feature, defect fix, refactor, or migration with an agreed verification plan.
- **Verification pairing:** combine implementation with a separately defined independent review using [Code Verification](https://github.com/Comdir2/Codex-code-verification).
- **Team enablement:** develop examples and training around bounded prompts, evidence quality, handoffs, and failure classification.
- **Workflow assessment:** identify where an existing coding-agent process lacks clear authority, isolation, reproducibility, or post-integration verification.

Each engagement requires a written scope covering the target repositories, authorized actions, deliverables, access boundaries, acceptance criteria, verification, confidentiality, schedule, and commercial terms. No deployment, live-system operation, destructive action, or access to private data is implied by an initial discussion.

## Starting packages

- **Workflow assessment — from USD 300:** review one repository's instructions, change process, test entry points, risk boundaries, and evidence gaps.
- **Custom repository setup — from USD 900:** adapt the maker-checker workflow, prompts, repository guidance, and verification commands to one codebase.
- **Team rollout — from USD 2,500:** cover multiple workflows, team onboarding, reusable examples, and an agreed adoption plan.

These are non-binding planning ranges, not an offer or quote. Final scope, price, taxes, payment method, availability, and legal terms must be agreed in writing before work begins.

## Requesting a discussion

Open a [GitHub issue](https://github.com/Comdir2/Codex-code-work/issues/new) with a title beginning `[Services]` and a non-confidential summary of:

- the repository type and technology stack;
- the implementation or workflow outcome sought;
- the important boundaries and environments;
- the desired form of evidence or deliverable.

Do not post credentials, proprietary code, customer information, private URLs, or commercial secrets in the public issue. A public issue is only a request to discuss scope; it is not an order, contract, quote, or authorization to begin work.

## Pricing and claims

The starting ranges above are not fixed prices. Any final price, availability, service level, warranty, or acceptance condition must be stated in a separate written proposal. The project makes no universal claim that a particular workflow, model, or amount of parallelism will improve speed, cost, correctness, or business results in every repository.

The reproducible evaluation corpus is published at [Codex Engineering Guardrails / benchmark](https://github.com/Comdir2/Codex-engineering-guardrails/tree/main/benchmark). Its deterministic validator establishes fixture integrity and known baselines, not model performance. A commercial evaluation must publish or privately deliver its exact run records and limitations.
